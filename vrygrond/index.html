Vrygrond Location -34.083801, 18.487244

Vrygrond is a socially and economically disadvantaged community home to roughly 10,000 men, women and children, although some sources say that the true population is as much as four times this figure. Situated near Muizenberg, on the False Bay coast about 20 kilometres from central Cape Town, it is the oldest informal settlement in the Western Cape. Trek fishermen used to  make a living from the sea having erected informal houses to live near the beach. Since then the people of Vrygrond have faced many obstacles and hardships to remain in the area. Development has taken place in Vrygrond and today there is a crèche, a library and a primary school and two Non-profit organizations doing phenomenal work. However, the persisting poverty has been a constant obstacle to growth.

Many families suffer from unemployment, leaving children without the needed support. Although Vrygrond is one of the oldest informal settlements in South Africa, it still faces many issues, but with much time and resources, these issues may be alleviated.
