Where It Started

Published by ben on July 17, 2018

Spread the love

The Beginning, November, 2017:

I was sitting at the supper table with my mom. The subject of my upcoming class 12 project emerged and I began thinking of what I wanted to achieve through my project. I have always liked business – making money, and I love learning new things. My mind went wild through random options which fitted these criteria when the idea hit me. I could raise money for a good cause – to help those in need and at the same time learn about my local community and history. On top of this I would learn great management skills, but best of all, I was going to give back to the surrounding local communities.

From this moment on I knew exactly what I planned to do. I was going to raise money through fundraisers, collect donations and then help a disadvantaged community through some project that I was going to think of. This turned out to be a lot more complicated than I had thought.

 

Figuring it out:

I began to brainstorm on how I was going to make my project a reality. The first step was to find a community in need, one that was not too big and one nearby. Living in South Africa made this possible but it would still not be an easy task. Isiyama Yethu came to mind, the township on the slopes of Hout Bay.

After setting my mind on a community I began to think how to get in touch. I thought of approaching churches or schools where I could possibly help out and get to know some people, after which I could ask specifically what their needs were. I had already thought of many things I could do but none of the ideas seemed feasible yet.

I sent emails to Isiyama Yethu Primary School but didn’t receive a reply. I then decided to drive there and talk to anyone I could so I decided to have a look at Google Maps street view to get familiar with the area. I was surprised to find that the area, although a township didn’t look to bad compared to other townships I had seen and heard of.

It was the new school year and I didn’t do much towards my project but one day I decided that it would be a smart idea perhaps to get in touch with other NGO’s to see what was already being done to help disadvantaged communities and what I could do to help. My mother told me about a women named Angela who was connected to the CCE and also started her own NGO named the Butterfly Art Project. This NGO was based in Vreygrond, a township near Retreat suffering from extreme poverty. I asked my mom if she could speak to Angela and arrange a meeting.
